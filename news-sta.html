Making Waves! The STA agree exciting new partnership with The Everything App

With over 90 years of experience in aquatic education, the STA understands the powerful connection between swimming and wellbeing better than most. As a UK-registered educational charity, the STA has built its reputation on promoting water safety and swimming skills, guided by their mission of teaching swimming and lifesaving to people of all ages and abilities. Now, through its latest partnership with The Everything App, the STA is leveraging this expertise to develop innovative programmes like "Couch to 1 Mile" - designed specifically for NHS staff and their families but delivered through leisure operators and swim schools nationwide.

A partnership for purpose

For the STA, supporting The Everything App aligns perfectly with their vision and values. The charity recognises the immense contribution NHS staff make to society, often at the expense of their own wellbeing. By partnering with The Everything App, the STA can leverage their extensive network to create meaningful change.

The partnership goes beyond just offering services – the STA is actively signposting their members and customers to support The Everything App, creating a ripple effect of support throughout the aquatic community. This collaborative approach reflects the STA's understanding that swimming isn’t just one of the most crucial life skills but can significantly impact the health and wellbeing of individuals who enjoy swimming as part of a healthy lifestyle.

At the heart of this partnership is the development of new, tailored programmes such as "Couch to 1 Mile" - a progressive swimming programme that takes participants from basic water confidence through to swimming a full mile. These programmes are specifically designed to be adopted and delivered by leisure operators and swim schools but with clear entry pathways and promotional pushes for the NHS staff and their families - recognising that many healthcare workers may be new to swimming or returning to the water after extended periods away from regular exercise.

The programme's accessibility and progressive structure allows participants to build confidence and fitness at their own pace - perfect for healthcare workers who may have limited time and varying fitness levels. This approach aligns with the STA's commitment to inclusive aquatic education while addressing the unique challenges faced by those working in high-stress healthcare environments.

A network of support for the NHS

The STA's role extends far beyond programme development. With their network of over 20,000 swimming-teaching members and 300+ Approved Training Centres, STA is uniquely positioned to mobilise the entire aquatic community in support of NHS staff. By encouraging their members to support The Everything App, the STA is creating a unified approach to wellbeing support across the UK's leisure and aquatic sectors.

The launch of The Everything App is a fantastic step forward in supporting the wellbeing of NHS staff and their families. As an educational charity dedicated to promoting health, safety, and enjoyment in aquatic activities, STA is committed to helping people of all ages and abilities stay active and healthy, which is why we are proud to support this initiative. By making physical activity more accessible and inclusive, the app will facilitate and open up new opportunities for NHS staff to look after their own physical and mental health, just as they take care of others every day. We look forward to working with all the sector partners to see the positive impact this initiative will have.

- Dave Candler, CEO of STA,

Julie Davis, founder of The Everything App adds: "Partnering with the STA brings incredible expertise and reach to The Everything App and we are hugely excited about this collaborative partnership. The development of new programmes such as the “'Couch to 1 Mile” embodies exactly what we're trying to achieve - making wellbeing support accessible and practical for NHS staff who give so much to others."

This partnership exemplifies how purpose-driven organisations can work together to create meaningful change - ensuring that those who dedicate their lives to caring for others receive the support and wellbeing opportunities they deserve.
